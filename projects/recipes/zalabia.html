Crispy Sweet Dumplings

Zalabia, also known as luqaimat, are small deep-fried dough balls that are crispy on the outside, soft inside, and soaked in syrup or dusted with powdered sugar. A favorite Egyptian treat!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water (approx.)
  • Oil for frying
  • Syrup or powdered sugar for topping

Instructions:

  1. Mix all dry ingredients, then add warm water gradually until you get a sticky dough.
  2. Cover and let it rise in a warm place for 45â€“60 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan. Use a spoon or piping bag to drop small portions into the oil.
  4. Fry until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Drain, then immediately dip in cold syrup or sprinkle with powdered sugar.
